Reactive • Walk-through • Mirrored Aluminum

It's time to Toast!

TOAST is a fully reactive toaster oven art installation. Dormant when empty, it awakens with human presence by glowing warmly, humming softly, and venting vapor—embodying the Burnt Toast Theory: small disruptions can reveal hidden harmony and transformation.

What is TOAST?

A futuristic, mirrored aluminum installation designed to evoke the form of a massive, glowing toaster oven resting on the playa. By day, it mirrors the vast sky; by night, it becomes a beacon of amber, gold, and violet.

Presence = ignition

The installation is activated entirely through human presence. As more people enter, light and sound intensify in a continuous “warming” curve — no performances, no music, just an appliance coming alive.

Burnt Toast Theory

What first appears ruined or flawed can become connection and unexpected joy. TOAST reframes disruption as a spark for transformation—made real through participation.

Universal, nonverbal access

No language, cultural knowledge, or prior Burning Man experience needed. Anyone can enter and become part of the work immediately.

How it works

One shared occupancy “brain” drives light, sound, and vapor—everything ramps smoothly.

Reactive lighting

  • LED rope coils surround the floor and ceiling inside TOAST
  • Warmth and brightness increase with occupancy
  • Dim or off when empty

Ambient sound

  • Soft electrical hum, gentle fan whirr, occasional relay clicks
  • Contained sound field (primarily inside)
  • No music, no voice, no bass projection

Vapor “burnt toast” moment

  • Fog machine triggers at a set “capacity” threshold
  • Short, controlled releases through upper vents
  • Syncs with a brief warmth/brightness peak

System logic (simple)

People count
↓
Lights warm + brighten
Sound layers fade in
Fog triggers at threshold
↓
Smooth fade back to idle

Built with low-voltage controls and weatherproof housings. Designed for safe flow: wide opening, rounded edges, marked thresholds, daily inspection.

Materials + build approach

Constructed from lightweight, reclaimed aluminum from a 1973 Airstream mounted on a steel frame. Mirrored surfaces use polished and reflective aluminum to mirror the vast playa.

Structure

Steel frame with reinforced joints and brackets; weatherproofed exterior; modular components for build/strike.

Power

Solar + battery system planned for low-voltage lighting, sensors, and sound—efficient and playa-ready.

Leave No Trace

Pre-fabrication off-playa, contained work area, daily MOOP sweeps, complete haul away and removal post-event.

Artist

Howdy! I'm Reza Tabesh, an interdisciplinary artist, filmmaker, and storyteller. Burning Man has shaped my life since 2017 and this will be my 8th burn. I create participatory, time-based experiences and document them through film and drone work worldwide.

Why TOAST

Bringing big ass art to the playa is a quest for me: to build something communal, welcoming, and alive — a place where people feel the shift from “spectator” to “co-creator.”
